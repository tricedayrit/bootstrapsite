Welcome to my website! I am Beatrice Dayrit: college student, artist, barista, and writer.

Originally from Bend, Oregon, I moved to Idaho to attend Boise State under the GIMM program. As of April 2020, my projected graduation date is in May 2022.

Through college, I've been working at Dutch Brothers, modeling, painting, and woodburning for fun and to earn some extra cash. Through the Great Quarantine of 2020, I took up woodworking and sculpting.

I have a little brother whom I adore. In my spare time, I enjoy hiking, cooking, and swimming. I've also enjoyed coding since high school, and have been working with bootstrap since I was fourteen.
If I'm not in the mountains or in a river, I'm usually in front of my computer writing. I'm currently working for fun on a textbook-style guide to Skyrim trivia. I also enjoy writing works of fiction as well as posting to forums about Star Wars and MCU (Marvel Cinematic Universe) discourse.
EDUCATION

I graduated with a 3.53 GPA from Mountain View High School in Bend, Oregon. Currently, I am a sophomore in college pursuing my bachelor's degree in GIMM at Boise State.

CAREER GOALS

Following graduation, I hope to work in design and development. I specifically enjoy creating sleek, user-friendly layouts for personal websites.

SKILLS

I am proficient in Microsoft Office products and Adobe products. I am best with Photoshop, as I have been working with it since I was ten. Further, I am fluent in HTML and CSS coding.

ART

I am a digital artist. I work in Photoshop CC 2019 and draw with a Wacom Intuos 2016. My favorite subjects are humans. I specialize in GIF art. I also enjoy woodburning art.

WORK EXPERIENCE

I entered the workforce at seventeen when I was hired at a candy company, Goody's. I remained with Goody's for two years as a shift lead until I went to college, and I then began work at Dutch Bros coffee.
Pokemon Trainer Mai

This was a gift to a friend, Elly, for her birthday. She wanted to see her character from a video game as a Pokemon trainer, complete with Pokemon of her choosing. I had tons of fun designing the character and creating an outfit for a Pokemon trainer-- the Pokeverse has some crazy outfits! Unfortunately, I had trouble with composition and anatomy, but by the end of the day I ended up having a great time designing it.I used Photoshop to complete everything in this piece. This was also my first venture into moving art; while I didn't personally animate the gifs used, I realized how nice it looked when there was a little something extra going on.

Always Find You

The same video game character Mai is a time traveler according to the game lore. She's a superhero of sorts, and here she is pictured traveling through time to save her ally from falling into Hell. This is my favorite piece I've done. Originally, the colors for the wisps coming off her were yellow, but I used the hue filter on Photoshop to change the colors into something softer. This is also the piece where I learned to draw hands and lighting; paying special attention to the bottom, there were tons of hand references I needed to figure out. It was frustrating, but I believe the payoff was worth it.

Lucien

This is the first piece I ever did as a digital artist. I actually didn't use Photoshop for this piece, as Photoshop did not have a reliable brush stabiliser when I first began working with it. I used Paint Tool SAI for this piece and had a blast with the brush textures, as evidenced by the scratchy pencil quality of the lines and the watercolor background texture.

The Getaway

This was a Christmas present for my friend Lou. This was the first piece I animated on my own; while it's not very intricate, I had thought at the time it was the best thing I'd ever done. At this time, I was still using Paint Tool SAI. After using the Timeline and frames to animate the coins in Photoshop, I made the switch.

What-If

I personally hated The Rise of Skywalker, but I'll admit that there seemed to be a really weird chemistry between Kylo Ren and Rey after it. This piece explores what could've happened after. This is probably the most advanced piece I've ever done, since the hair, eyes, lips, trees, waterfalls, and sky are all animated. I hand-animated this frame-by-frame in Photoshop.

Stolen Moments

Like most of my pieces, this was commissioned by a friend of mine, Thea. It features Kylo Ren and Rey if Rey were secretly long-lost royalty. My goal for this piece was to challenge color scheme; purple and dark colors are traditionally not considered colors that evoke warmth, so I attempted it through expression. I'm personally proud of the expressions of the characters in this piece. Further, the stars in the background were the first smooth animations I'd done in an art piece.

The Dark Side

This is how I did want The Rise of Skywalker to go. The trailers of Dark Rey that Lucasfilms dropped in the months before the movie's release made me think that she would turn to the dark side, Kylo Ren would turn to the light side, and she would kill him. The lighting was so much fun, as well as coming up with a new technique I'd never done before to draw the lightsabers. What I'm personally proud of is the look in Rey's eyes as she realizes what she's about to do, and the exploding legacy saber. I had a blast (no pun intended) with the composition.
